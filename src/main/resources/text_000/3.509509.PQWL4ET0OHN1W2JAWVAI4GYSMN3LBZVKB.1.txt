This letter of intent ("Agreement") effective when executed by all the parties hereto, will evidence the current mutual intent, as set forth in Article I below, of Enron North America Corp., a Delaware corporation ("ENA") and J.M. Huber Corporation, a New Jersey corporation ("Huber"), for the capacity release of certain transportation gas capacity and a gas purchase/sale transaction (the "Project"). This Agreement is intended to set forth certain basic terms of the understanding reached to date and to serve as a basis for further discussions and negotiations among the Parties with respect to the Project. Any such binding agreement will only arise upon the negotiation, execution and delivery of a mutually satisfactory definitive agreements and the satisfaction of the conditions set forth therein, including the approval of such agreements by each Party.  The matters set forth in Article II do constitute binding agreements of the Parties.
In consideration of the rights and obligations of the Parties hereunder, and other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which being hereby acknowledged by the Parties, the Parties hereby agree as follows:
ARTICLE I
THE PROJECT

Definitive Agreements. The Parties will meet to discuss the Project and the necessary agreements relating thereto to work towards the execution of definitive agreements by October 18, 2001.  It is understood that the Parties are not, by virtue of this Agreement undertaking any liability with regard to the Project; any such undertaking would be addressed in a subsequent definitive agreement.
Non-Binding. Neither Party is obligated hereunder to enter into any definitive agreement with the other, with respect to the Project.

3.  Release of El Paso Capacity.  ENA would permanently release 30,000 MMBtu of transportation capacity (the “Capacity”) on El Paso Natural Gas Company (“El Paso”) to Huber for a term commencing on November 1, 2001 and ending on May 31, 2006.  Such release would be a prearranged capacity release transaction at the maximum rate specificed in El Paso’s FERC approved transportation rates.  
 
4.  Re-Release of El Paso Capacity.  Huber would release the Capacity to ENA for successive one (1) year terms during the term of the gas purchase as set forth in Paragraph 5 below in order for ENA to meet its obligations under such gas purchase.  Such release would be a prearranged capacity release transaction at the maximum rate specificed in El Paso’s FERC approved transportation rates.  


5.  Gas Purchase.  ENA would purchase Huber gas in the following packages:


DCQ: The quantity of gas scheduled by El Paso at PG&E Topock based on a nomination by ENA of 30,000 MMBtu per day at PG&E Topock
Price:  Gas Daily Midpoint Index for PG&E Large Packages for the applicable period less $0.05 per MMBtu
Delivery Point: PG&E Topock
Period of Delivery: November 1, 2001 through May 30, 2006
  

DCQ:  The remaining quantity of gas not scheduled by El Paso at PG&E Topock in Section 5.A. above and alternatively nominated by ENA into Northwest Pipeline
Price: Gas Daily Midpoint Index for Northwest Pipeline Wyoming Pool, for the applicable period less $0.10 per MMBtu
Delivery Point:  El Paso Williams- Ignacio Plant Tailgate
Period of Delivery:  November 1, 2001 until the first full month following the reallocation of receipt point capacity by El Paso Natural Gas Company


DCQ:  Up to 10,000 MMBtu per day
Price: Inside FERC El Paso San Juan Index less $.10 per MMBtu
Delivery Point: El Paso Williams- Ignacio Plant Tailgate
Period of Delivery: November 1, 2001 until the first full month following the reallocation of receipt point capacity by El Paso Natural Gas Company


ENA and Huber would utilize the pricing structure set forth above to create a “wellhead sale” from Huber to ENA under this transaction in which the Delivery Points under 5.A, 5.B and 5.C above would be converted to the outlet of the Durango compressor station and ENA would manage all of Huber’s logistics and scheduling for the gas packages downstream of such compressor station.  The prices set forth in 5.A, 5.B and 5.C would be decreased by the appropriate gathering charges, fuel charges and any other charges which are applicable under Huber’s gathering agreements for this gas.  The gas purchase/sale set forth in this Paragraph 3 would be executed under that certain Enfolio® Master Firm Purchase/Sale agreement between ENA and Huber dated August 1, 2000.

If, in the sole opinion and discretion of either Party, such discussion is unsuccessful, such Party may submit the Dispute to mediation.
